🚨 Critical Issue: CPA is 73% above Target

The beard campaign's actual CPA of ₹1,475.63 is nearly double the target of ₹850. This is the single biggest constraint on the beard vertical. Google's algorithm cannot find enough users who convert at the ₹850 CPA threshold, so it severely limits spend. The campaign gets only 7.9 purchases/day despite having a ₹25,000/day budget — spending just ₹11,600/day of it.

Root cause: The beard purchase intent audience may be naturally smaller or require higher bids to reach. The ₹850 target may have been set based on outdated data or aspirational targets rather than what the algorithm can actually achieve.

Tier 2-3 Analysis

Both Tier 2-3 campaigns are small experimental campaigns (₹4k/day budget) targeting India minus 23 Tier-1 cities. They use Maximize Conversions (no Target CPA), which is why they show as "Eligible" unlike the main campaigns.

📊
Low Purchase-to-Install Ratio in Tier 2-3
Beard Tier(2-3): Only 8 purchases from 610 installs = 1.3% conversion rate
Hair Tier(2-3): Only 36 purchases from 377 installs = 9.5% conversion rate

The install-to-purchase conversion rate in Tier 2-3 areas is much lower than Tier 1, suggesting these users install but don't purchase. The effective CPA per purchase is extremely high (₹6,940 for beard). ROI of the Beard Tier(2-3) campaign is questionable.
💡
Both campaigns use Maximize Conversions without any CPA guard-rail
While this explains why they're "Eligible" (no bid limit), it also means Google has no cost constraint and can spend on any inventory. Adding a Target CPA of ₹1,000-1,200 for hair or ₹3,000 for beard could help quality over quantity, though volume will drop further.

⚠️ Issues Found — Prioritised

🔴 Critical (2) 🟡 Warning (3) 🔵 Improvement (3)
🚨
CRITICAL #1 — UAC-Hair-01 & Beard-01: "Limited by Bid Strategy" Root Cause of Under-spend
What's happening: UAC-Hair-01 actual CPA (₹542.69) is 13% over the ₹480 target. UAC-Beard-01 actual CPA (₹1,475.63) is 73% over the ₹850 target. When actual CPA exceeds the target, Google's algorithm becomes conservative and restricts the campaign from bidding on more inventory — it "protects" you from overpaying.

Impact: ₹62,870/day of budget is sitting idle (Hair-01: ₹50,073/day + Beard-01: ₹13,392/day). Over 30 days, this is ₹1.89Cr of unspent budget potential.

Resolution: Either (a) gradually relax Target CPA to match actual performance, or (b) work to lower actual CPA through creative improvements to meet the current target. See Scaling Strategy tab.
🚨
CRITICAL #2 — Hair Policy Violations (3 of 4 campaigns affected) Restricts Ad Serving
What's happening: UAC-Hair-01 (Hair-English ad group), UAC-Hair-Tier(2-3)-01 (Hair-English ad group) are flagged "Limited by Policy." This means some creative assets are violating Google's healthcare/medical advertising policies — typically unsubstantiated hair regrowth claims, before/after comparisons, or medical terminology without disclaimers.

Impact: Ads cannot serve to the full eligible audience. This directly reduces impression share and NC volume.

Resolution: Review the specific policy violation reason in the ad group details, update the flagged creatives to remove policy-violating language, and resubmit for review.
⚠️
WARNING #3 — Low-rated Assets Dragging Quality Across Campaigns
UAC-Hair-01: 3 Low descriptions, 2 Low headlines, 3 Low videos (0:25, 0:59, 0:07 lengths)
UAC-Beard-01: 3 Low descriptions, 3 Low headlines, multiple Low-rated videos

Low-rated assets reduce the algorithm's creative confidence. Google prioritises serving Best/Good assets and may suppress the entire ad group's delivery when too many Low assets exist. These should be replaced with fresh creatives modelled on the Best-performing ones.
⚠️
WARNING #4 — UAC-Beard-01: Target CPA May Be Structurally Unachievable
The ₹850 target CPA for beard purchases has a 73% gap vs actual performance (₹1,475). This suggests the ₹850 target was either set aspirationally or based on an earlier period when costs were lower. The beard vertical likely has a naturally higher CPA given smaller purchase intent audience. A CPA Simulator check on this campaign is highly recommended before deciding whether to raise the target or restructure the campaign.
⚠️
WARNING #5 — Beard Tier(2-3): Very Low Purchase ROI
Only 8 beard purchases from 610 installs (1.3% conversion rate) in 30 days. At ₹6,940/purchase, this campaign's ROI is extremely questionable. Compare: UAC-Hair-Tier(2-3) gets 36 purchases from 377 installs (9.5% conversion) — much better ratio. The beard Tier 2-3 audience may simply not be a strong purchase market. Budget reallocation should be evaluated.
💡
IMPROVEMENT #6 — View-Through Conversion Optimisation is OFF on All Campaigns
All 4 campaigns have View-Through Conversion Optimisation turned off. View-through attribution (users who saw an ad, didn't click, but later converted) is significant — Hair-01 alone recorded 2,760+ view-through conversions. Enabling this setting allows Google to factor in view-through signals when optimising bids, potentially improving reach to high-LTV audiences who convert via multi-touch journeys.
💡
IMPROVEMENT #7 — No Data Feed Connected to Any Campaign
All 4 campaigns show "Data feed: Not connected." A product/content feed can help Google create more personalised ad experiences (especially for images and native ad formats) and improve targeting signals. For Man Matters, a product feed containing SKU-level data (hair kit, beard kit, subscription products) could improve conversion rates by showing more contextually relevant ads.
💡
IMPROVEMENT #8 — Android-Only; No iOS Campaigns
All UAC campaigns are exclusively targeting Android devices. If Man Matters has iOS users (Apple App Store presence), a separate iOS UAC campaign could unlock an additional audience segment. iOS users in India tend to have higher purchasing power (Tier 1 demographics), potentially with better CPA economics for Hair products.

🚀 Scaling Strategy — Experiments to Go from 100 to 150+ NC/day

The following experiments are ranked by expected impact and ease of execution. All are non-destructive changes that can be tested without touching the existing campaigns.

1
Fix Hair Policy Violations → Immediate Volume Unlock
🔴 Highest Priority

The problem: Both hair campaigns have ad groups "limited by policy," directly suppressing impression volume and NC delivery.

  • Go to UAC-Hair-01 → Ad Groups → Hair-English → Check the "Policy" status detail
  • Identify which specific assets are flagged (descriptions, headlines, or videos)
  • Revise the copy: remove unverified medical claims ("regrows hair in X weeks"), replace with benefit language ("see visible improvement with regular use")
  • Resubmit assets for policy review (typically 1–3 business days)
  • Apply same fix to UAC-Hair-Tier(2-3)-01
Expected Outcome: 10–20% increase in impression share on hair campaigns. If Hair-01 is currently running at 50% budget utilisation partly due to policy, fixing policy could add 15–25 NC/day incrementally.
2
Relax UAC-Hair-01 Target CPA from ₹480 → ₹520 (Gradual Test)
🔴 Highest Priority

The problem: The ₹480 Target CPA is too tight — actual CPA is ₹542.69 (13% over). This forces Google to hold back spend, wasting ₹50k/day of available budget.

  • Use the "Simulate campaign changes" tool in UAC-Hair-01 to preview volume at ₹510, ₹520, and ₹540
  • If simulator shows >15% volume uplift at ₹520, create a new campaign UAC-Hair-02 with ₹520 target CPA and ₹20,000–30,000/day budget
  • Keep UAC-Hair-01 unchanged (don't raise its CPA — risk of disrupting 92 NC/day)
  • Let UAC-Hair-02 run for 14 days before evaluating
Expected Outcome: If UAC-Hair-02 achieves even 50% budget utilisation at ₹520 CPA with a ₹25k/day budget, that's ~48 additional NC/day. A 30k/day budget → 57 NC/day, getting you to 150 NC/day total.
3
Creative Refresh — Replace All Low-Rated Assets
🟡 High Priority

The problem: Both Hair-01 and Beard-01 have multiple "Low" rated assets. These drag quality, reduce Google's confidence, and dilute the overall creative mix.

  • Hair-01: Replace 3 Low descriptions, 2 Low headlines, 3 Low videos (0:25, 0:59, 0:07 lengths)
  • Beard-01: Replace 3 Low descriptions, 3 Low headlines, multiple Low-rated videos
  • New creative themes to test: Reward/savings angle (top-performing theme based on Best assets), social proof ("50,000+ men transformed"), problem-solution framing
  • For videos: Focus on 0:15–0:30 length (best performing range for UAC)
  • Add assets as new ad groups first to A/B test before replacing the originals
Expected Outcome: Better creative quality → improved install-to-purchase CVR → lower actual CPA → algorithm serves more. Even a 5–10% CPA improvement on Hair-01 could close the gap to the ₹480 target and unlock 10–20 NC/day.
4
New Ad Group in UAC-Hair-01 — Hair-English-02 (Separate Creative Set)
🟡 High Priority

The strategy: Rather than editing the existing Hair-English ad group (which is the workhorse at 92 NC/day), create a second ad group within UAC-Hair-01 with a completely fresh creative set. This lets Google's algorithm discover new winning creatives without risking the existing performance.

  • Create "Hair-English-02" ad group in UAC-Hair-01
  • Upload 3–5 new headlines, 2–3 new descriptions, 3–5 new videos, 4–5 new images
  • Creative brief: Focus on app exclusivity + rewards + expert guidance messaging (themes that scored "Best" in existing assets)
  • Monitor for 7–14 days — Google will auto-allocate budget to better-performing ad group
Expected Outcome: If the new creative set achieves lower CPA than Hair-English, the campaign's overall CPA could drop below ₹480 — removing the bid strategy limitation and allowing full budget utilisation.
5
Beard-01: Run CPA Simulator + Incremental CPA Test
🟡 Medium Priority

The problem: UAC-Beard-01's actual CPA (₹1,475) is 73% above the ₹850 target. The beard category is significantly harder to scale at the current target.

  • Use "Simulate campaign changes" on UAC-Beard-01 to see projected volume at ₹1,000, ₹1,200, ₹1,500 CPA
  • If ₹1,200 shows 2x+ volume, create UAC-Beard-02 with Target CPA ₹1,200 and ₹10,000/day budget
  • Run for 21 days and evaluate actual CPA vs target
  • Do NOT raise target on existing Beard-01 — keep it as the efficiency benchmark
Expected Outcome: Even at ₹1,200 CPA, if beard conversion improves from 7.9/day to 15/day, that is a meaningful NC contribution. Long-term goal: understand true beard CPA floor and set targets accordingly.
6
Enable View-Through Conversion Optimisation (Low Risk Test)
🟣 Medium Priority

All 4 campaigns have View-Through Conversion Optimisation turned OFF. Enabling it allows Google to value and bid on users who see ads without clicking but eventually convert.

  • Start with UAC-Beard-Tier(2-3)-01 (safest — smallest, no other issues)
  • Enable View-Through Conversion Optimisation and monitor for 14 days
  • If CPA holds or improves, roll out to UAC-Hair-Tier(2-3)-01, then the main campaigns
Expected Outcome: Google can reach more users in the consideration phase. View-through signals may improve the algorithm's user targeting, reducing CPA and increasing conversion volume over time.
7
Scale UAC-Hair-Tier(2-3)-01 Budget After Policy Fix
🟢 Medium Priority

Hair Tier(2-3) is getting 36 purchases/30 days at ₹1,548/purchase from Tier 2-3 areas. While the CPA is higher than Tier 1, the install-to-purchase rate (9.5%) is reasonable. Once the policy block is fixed (Experiment 1), this campaign could scale with additional budget.

  • First fix the policy issue in Hair-English ad group
  • After 7 days of Eligible status, test increasing budget from ₹4,000 to ₹8,000/day
  • Monitor CPA carefully — Tier 2-3 users may have lower LTV
  • Consider adding a ₹1,200 Target CPA guard-rail to prevent runaway costs
Expected Outcome: Could add 3–8 incremental NC/day from Tier 2-3 hair segment. Not a volume multiplier but meaningful incremental contribution.

30-Day Scaling Roadmap

Week 1: Fix Issues (Zero Risk)
Resolve policy violations in Hair-English ad groups (Hair-01 + Hair Tier 2-3). Replace all Low-rated assets with fresh creatives. Run CPA Simulator on both Hair-01 and Beard-01 to understand volume potential at higher CPA levels.
Week 2: Launch New Experiments
Launch UAC-Hair-02 (new campaign) with ₹520 Target CPA and ₹20,000/day budget. Add Hair-English-02 ad group to UAC-Hair-01 with fresh creatives. Enable View-Through Optimisation on UAC-Beard-Tier(2-3)-01.
Week 3: Evaluate + Iterate
Review UAC-Hair-02 performance. If CPA < ₹540, increase its budget to ₹30,000/day. If Hair-English-02 showing better CPA, begin replacing underperforming assets in Hair-English. Launch UAC-Beard-02 test at ₹1,200 Target CPA.
Week 4: Scale Winners
Scale budget on any campaign/ad group achieving CPA at or below target. Target: 130–150 NC/day total UAC. Continue creative refresh cycle. Consider launching iOS UAC campaigns if Android scaling stalls.
